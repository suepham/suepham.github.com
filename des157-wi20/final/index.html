ABOUT LAW

Plastic pollution is an increasing issue. It is estimated that every minute, 1 million water bottles are sold, and the majority of the bottles are sent to landfill. These bottles may end up in oceans that kill marine life and destroy their habitat. The bottles sitting on the landfill release harmful toxins and chemicals into the Earth, and it could end up in the soil that grows our food. Plastic takes centuries to degrade, and they usually break up into microplastics that ends up in our every day food and water.

Many do not realize the amount of energy and resources that it takes to make each plastic bottle. Making the plastic requires oil pulled from the Earth, and it depletes the natural resources we have available. Making the plastic uses a lot of energy and water, and it is much more sustinable to drink the water itself without the bottle. The amount of emissions it takes to transport was also harms the environment. Even though these bottles can be recycled, many people continue to throw the plastic into landfill.

OUR MISSION

We strive to educate the population on the production of plastic bottles. Our experience places the user into the entire lifecycle of plastic bottles from when they are pulled from oil in the Earth to their disposal process. We hope to reduce the amount of plastic in the world and encourage recycling. Plastic is harmful to both the natural world and species that live on it. It is an issue that needs better resources and solutions.
